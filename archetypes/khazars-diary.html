Highlights

This is an allies toolbox deck using multiple unique allies. The idea is to put Khazar's Diary (Endless Night) into play, breed a bit using The Embrace, defend by putting some allies into play and wait until Khazar's Diary triggers. At this point, the dead allies come back into play as wraith to stealth & bleed the successive preys out of the game.

The high recursion using Sudario Refraction is a strong point of the deck, allowing it to adapt its defence to the table context. It is very passice in the game beginning and uses Deflection and Delaying Tactics to defend a much as possible.

The deck is focused into bringing as many minions into play as possible, and relies a lot on the allies and their capacities. As such, it does have only limited combat, bleed or stealth modules. However, it hopes to quickly bring into play good rush or intercept allies like Carlton Van Wyk, Ossian and Shambling Hordes to improve its defence.

Shambling Hordes is a particularily strong rush minion: it is not Unique (adn as such does not add a counter to Khazar's Diary as it burns), but it is strong enough a defence to be included nonetheless. And given the rest of the deck mechanics and the numerous Liquidation, it will easily find an ally in the ash heap.

The Unmasking and FBI Special Affairs Division help making all these allies stronger defenders when they come into play.

Tips & Tricks

Jake Washington is an excellent device in this deck as burning him in any way will add one counter on Khazar's Diary easily.

This variant uses The Parthenon to get to play more masters and improve its bloat with Liquidation and Vessel. Destroying some Blood Doll using Vessel has a bit of value in this deck, as Gregory Winter and Young Bloods can stack over the rush allies to bring a quite strong blood attrition around the table.

Repo Man is a cheap unique ally, and, with his 1 life, dies pretty quickly if he blocks. Which is the point when one uses Khazar's Diary.

Variants

Iñaki Puigdollers's deck from 2009 uses Change of Target to help protect the minions — even the allies — from unforseen blocks and some Tupdog in the crypt to avoid repeating minions and provide a bit more in terms of defence: they can not act as they are no Tremere Antitribu in the deck, but they can still block — and provide one more minion to bring back as a wraith once Khazar's Diary kicks in.

Roberto Venturi's deck from 2011 uses Anarch Convert for a better rotation, and to make all these Giovanni Anarch so they can use Cry Wolf, an ally exceptionnaly well suited for the Khazar's Diary, as it is unique and burns automatically. It provides the same kind of rush defence Ossian and Shambling Hordes do in the example deck.

Enrico De Stefano's deck from 2018 uses a dozen Draeven Softfoot and their burn option to quickly bring more allies into the ash heap, making it easier to get Shambling Hordes into play. It uses more The Embrace and Khazar's Diary as the idea is to trigger the Diary sooner, and bring all these Draeven Softfoot back as wraith with the new born vampires, for a faster, more aggressive version of the archetype.

Errata

Pentex™ Subversion has been modified by an errata in September 2019. It does not prevent a vampire to act anymore, so it is less powerful and should maybe not be included in the deck anymore. Still its ability to disable a strong blocker is useful for the lunge.

Anthelios, The Red Star has been banned in 2016. It should not be included in the deck anymore. Its absence makes the inclusion of The Parthenon and so many Master cards questionable.
