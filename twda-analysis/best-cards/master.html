Master

Pentex™ Subversion (1293 decks)

Before its errata in september 2019, Pentex was played in over half of tournament decks and it was the most played card overall. It used to prevent the target minion from acting too, and that made it the most versatile card of the game. In the endgame its power is still worthwhile though: disabling a specific minion on the prey side can be key to victory, especially for decks that do not include a lot of stealth.

Dreams of the Sphinx (1100 decks)

Loved for its ubiquitousness, the Dreams provides either two pool (and can even accelerate your setup if played in the beginning) or help the deck rotation in critical moments by giving a two cards rotation.

Giant's Blood (955 decks)

Even if it is only once per game, it brings more blood on the table than any other card, with the notable exception of Golconda: Inner Peace. It is often played in a single exemplary: with a bit of luck it brings a definitive advantage, otherwise it prevents an opponent to play it himself.

Vessel (847 decks)

With its cousin Blood Doll, it is the most used leech cards. Both enable a 1 point leech per turn, but also provide the ability to give some blood back to a thirsty vampire, which can be key to avoid hunts and keep one’s ABC high. Compared to Blood Doll, its cost is higher (it takes a full turn to kick in and costs 1 pool, making it a two points bloat difference when compared to Blood Doll), but it is more secure as it cannot be destroyed and, more importantly, it is a Trifle: a second full fledged Master card can still be played after.

Villein (756 decks)

Before its errata in 2018, it permitted to take back as much blood as desired from the target vampire with no upper limit. Its Trifle trait made it one of the best leech option, besting the Minion Tap. The errata made Minion Tap relevant again, so no doubt the play count between the two cards will evolve accordingly.

Direct Intervention (694 decks)

The ability to cancel any minion card is extremely valuable. Of course the 1 pool cost ensures it cannot be abused too much, still keeping it in hand to cripple a lunge sequence has proven to be a definitive edge for winning a game.

Blood Doll (552 decks)

Similar to Vessel in its effect, it is not a Trifle, so it does not fit in all decks depending on the number of Master cards. Even if it can be destroyed by a Vessel, it is two turns faster as it is immediately usable when played and costs zero pool.

Information Highway (542 decks)

An excellent crypt accelerator at no cost, it is played both with big capacity crypts, but also with weenie crypts at 1-2 capacity to make it possible to draw and get out a vampire in a single turn. Even if it is Unique, some decks include it in multiple exemplaries to improve their chances to draw it early on.

Powerbase: Montreal (491 decks)

A good bloat device that generates one pool per turn at no cost. It is a no-brainer in decks able to defend it. It can also be a useful piece of bargain when dealing with other Methusalahs, as it can easily be borrowed as part of a deal.

Wider View (451 decks)

A cheap Trifle that provides some rotation to the crypt. For a lot of crypts, this is a useful asset in the mid game to help fetch a third vampire easily. It only takes a slot and is even guaranteed to generate one pool at some point.

Fame (409 decks)

A key card for combat heavy decks, usually used on the prey's minions, it provides excellent pool damage. The fact that it is Unique means it can not easily be abused over and over again, though.

Archon Investigation (392 decks)

A terrible deterrent against too powerful bleeders. A must have for decks who do not have access to bounces, it can be included in virtually any deck just to discourage the 6-7 bleed scores d and r can provide.

Wash (388 decks)

Countering Master cards is often useful — the best move is to counter a bloat card like Villein, which is equivalent to a serious bleed. Moreover, other powerful Master cards can be countered this way too.

Jake Washington (350 decks)

A good way to bring back some blood on a vampire, it can also provide protection by blocking an action and is a key combination with War Ghoul and Khazar's Diary (Endless Night), as it brings a unique Ally in the game without even risking an action someone could block.

The Parthenon (340 decks)

A useful tool as it enable the play of more Master cards at no cost, it is often combined with vampires providing yet another Master action: Anson, Nana Buruku, Cybele, Huitzilopochtli and Isanwayen.

Perfectionist (337 decks)

An easy to get some pool back on a vampire at no cost. Many actions go through unblocked and unreacted to, so it tends to bring a pool back on the vampire at least every other turn.

The Rack (332 decks)

One of the best tool available to bring some blood back on a vampire, it works beautifully with Blood Doll or Vessel. It needs to be defended to be kept in play, but such a good blood generator at no cost is a bargain.

Sudden Reversal (322 decks)

Very similar to Wash, it is not a Trifle but, on the other hand, it does not let the target play another Master, so it is a bit more efficient for stopping a bloat to come through. So, if there is room for a full fledged Master in the deck, it is a marginally better option.

Monastery of Shadows (317 decks)

A costly but efficient location. The stealth can very well make the difference between life or death for the prey, and the bonus hand size is nothing to frown upon. It is a little bit better in decks using Parity Shift as it can get the pool down just enough to be able to play the vote against one's prey.

The Coven (284 decks)

A nice way to bring some blood back on the table, is it a useful tool against rush and grinder decks. Even if it does not come along so often, the fact that it helps the whole table can really slow down these particular archetypes.

Anarch Troublemaker (280 decks)

A powerful tool for powerbleed constructs. Its ability to destroy an equipment can be useful, but it is mostly included for its ability to lock vampires, and it is a key card for bleed decks without stealth.

Ashur Tablets (279 decks)

A subtle card: it is a decent bloating device but its key ability is to allow some recursion in the deck. The ability to adapt one's deck content to the table context is very powerful if the deck has enough options for it to make a real difference. Played in conjunction with some cards to add Master action, it becomes tremendously efficient. Decks that use it typically include a dozen of them, but getting over 20 exemplaries is not unheard of.

Secure Haven (272 decks)

Although Unique, it is a solid counter against rush decks. Other options are required to really defend against them as this can only protect one vampire, but still this is a vampire one would not take down easily.

Smiling Jack, The Anarch (264 decks)

This is a good damage dealing option for Wall decks. As they tend to be low on pool damage, this helps them ousting the table in timely fashion.

Golconda: Inner Peace (245 decks)

Althouhg loosing a vampire seems like too high a price to pay, this is the best bloat card of the game. Played or 10 or even 11 capacity vampire, that's a lot of pool — moreover if Minion Tap has been played on the vampire the turn before. Also, it can always be played for 2 pool damages on the prey, and even used in nast combinations with Summon History, The Eternal Mask or Graverobbing.

Zillah's Valley (217 decks)

An excellent crypt accelerator provide one has big capacity vampires and enough pool to use it. It is a very good combination with Villein or Minion Tap, but also helps get some pool out fast to play a Parity Shift on one's prey.

The Barrens (202 decks)

Although not as versatile as Dreams of the Sphinx, it is a good additional tool to help card rotation at no cost.

KRCG News Radio (193 decks)

A good permanent intercept card at a decent price, it can even be used to help an ally around the table. As it is Unique, other locations can be included along it to improve intercept, in order of frequency WRMH Talk Radio, Channel 10, The Rumor Mill, Tabloid Newspaper and Wall Street Night, Financial Newspaper .

Misdirection (168 decks)

A nice tool for powerbleed constructs, it helps to get some bleed through when one has no stealth. Although a bit expansive, it is often a key card for the lunge in such decks.

Haven Uncovered (168 decks)

An excellent device for combat-oriented decks. Broader than a rush action like Bum's Rush, it lets all the minions rush the target. This is a good way to quickly overwhelm a "Strike: combat ends" defensive module, plus it will take an action to remove, slowing the opponent even more.

Minion Tap (159 decks)

An excellent bloat device, it helps to get multiple big capacity vampires out at a reasonnable cost. Even better with crypt accelerators like Zillah's Valley and Information Highway, it has long been outclassed by Villein, until the errata of 2018 setting an upper limit of 5 blood on it. Now both options have their pro and cons and Minion Tap makes a comeback.

Liquidation (157 decks)

An excellent bloat device, its side-effect of card discard can be a bummer, as many decks rely on the fact that even just 2 exemplaries of a given card are enough to draw it at some point in the game. However, used in conjunction with Ashur Tablets, Emerald Legionnaire or Sudario Refraction, it becomes unvaluable.
