o Obfuscate

o provides the best stealth in the game, as its name suggests.

Lost in Crowds (468 decks)

The best stealth money can buy, played in almost all o constructs.

Elder Impersonation (403 decks)

This block denial modifier comes in very handy against a single minion. It works against all minions and vampires and can bypass even high levels of stacked intercept.

Faceless Night (403 decks)

A single unlocked minion can be hard to get around if multiple actions are in order. This is an excellent tool in this case as it lets you lock the defending minion on the first block failure. Furthermore, it will work as a block denial against allies.

Swallowed by the Night (371 decks)

Decks with an actual combat module along o include it because of its versatility. Controlling the range is always an issue and the maneuver option comes in handy. Even with a thin combat module, going to long range can still be useful to avoid some damage.

Cloak the Gathering (319 decks)

An additional source of stealth that becomes an excellent tool when not all vampires have o. It can also be stacked, by the vampire doing the action himself, then by another vampire with O.

Spying Mission (247 decks)

An additional stealth modifier that can be stacked over more widespread ones, it is also a decent counter against bounces: it allows one to suppress a high damage bleed that has been redirected to its grand-prey, which is actually quite a common situation.

Forgotten Labyrinth (229 decks)

Especially useful in decks with a political module, it gives such a high bonus to undirected actions that they become virtually unblockable. Its uselessness for directed actions still makes it absent from a lot of o modules.

Veil the Legions (193 decks)

Less widely used than Cloak the Gathering because it offers no direct stealth on an action, its efficiency increases in decks with more low capacity vampires like weenie or swarm constructs. A vampire with O can give +1 stealth to multiple consecutive bleeds, a nice setup for a lunge move.
