Akunanse

Akunanse and their rare discipline w provide a stronger defence than the usual i builds.

This clan has two main archetypes: the Akunanse Wall, using w along i for a sturdy wall, and Team Jacob using the infamous Tunnel Runner.

Highlights

Uchenna (31 decks)

A Magaji with superior WFI for just 7 pool: definitely a good deal, plus her ability may be useful in tense situations although 1 blood to draw a card is a high price to pay. The fact that she is Magaji let her play the excellent No Secrets From the Magaji.

Mapatano Utando (22 decks)

A good master for Akunanse decks — it can speed up the build up in the beginning, but also avoid pool loss regularly, at no cost. Not really a game turner, but gaining one or two turns or 3 pools or more is definitely efficient.

Reliquary: Akunanse Remains (14 decks)

A good equipment: +1 bleed for 1 pool is good, plus the discipline boost is excellent for low capacity vampires.

Tunnel Runner (13 decks)

He can be used as the main minion of the deck for good stealthed bleed, his ability can be used more than once in a turn, allowing it to get +2 stealth. He is expensive, though — he becomes really efficient partnered with Jacob Fermor in the Jacob's Team archetype.

Predator's Communion (32 decks)

Wake and intercept bundled in a single card: an excellent reaction for any intercept module. This card, combined with the combat-efficient f discipline fo the clan, explains the success of wall archetypes in the Akunanse clan.

Invoking the Beast (211 decks)

A good combat card at no cost: additional damage, plus a handy press that combines very well with Carrion Crows

Predator's Transformation (20 decks)

Not a very good stealth card, but the only one available for Akunanse. The superior effect may come in handy against weenie constructs.

Devil-Channel: Hands (15 decks)

An excellent combat cards, adding aggravated damage to an already efficient combat module.
