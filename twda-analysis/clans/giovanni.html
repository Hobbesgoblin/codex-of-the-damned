Giovanni

The Giovanni and their rare n discipline, combined with the fact that they have d as a clan discipline, come up with two original allies archetypes: the Khazar's Diary, centered on allies and Khazar's Diary (Endless Night), and Shambling Hordes, an allies archetype using Shambling Hordes.

Multiple powerbleed constructs have been seen, but nothing very stable. The combination of Call of the Hungry Dead and Seduction is strong, and Guillaume Giovanni gave birth to interesting deck lists, though all quite different.

Highlights

Call of the Hungry Dead (131 decks)

Played in most n decks, it is an excellent block denial card. Combined with Seduction, it can get any action through without even fighting for stealth.

Spectral Divination (114 decks)

A very versatile card, although the 1 blood cost is a bit high.

Sudario Refraction (83 decks)

The most played Giovanni card. Its very rare recursion ability enables the Giovanni decks to adapt their card flow to the table context using recursion, which is an excellent edge.

Lia Milliner (78 decks)

d and n for only 3 pool: a bargain. She can even find her place in mono d builds.

Gloria Giovanni (74 decks)

Superior D and n for only 4 pool: another bargain. She finds an easy place in mono d builds.

Isabel Giovanni (68 decks)

Superior DN for only 5 pool, it is often the perfect disciplines combination for Giovanni decks, so Isabel is often played in multiple copies.

Tye Cooper (66 decks)

Played in many Giovanni decks, even when they are not centred on allies, he is very resilient and can be good defence.

Spiritual Intervention (64 decks)

An excellent defensive combat card, providing a combat ends effect for no cost at superior N, and still a useful dodge for inferior n.

Powerbase: Cape Verde (57 decks)

A generalized Blood Doll, an efficient bloat device.

Shambling Hordes (53 decks)

An excellent ally with solid combat abilities given its strength. A bit tricky to play or include in a deck, as it needs an ally or vampire in the ash heap already.

Leonardo, Mortician (42 decks)

A cheap ally with a nice ability. Often included to fuel Shambling Hordes or Khazar's Diary (Endless Night).

Andrea Giovanni (42 decks)

An excellent abilities for decks relying a lot on the N discipline, like those using Shambling Hordes.

The Path of Bone (39 decks)

A good way to reduce blood consumption in n decks. As is the case with many Path cards, it is quite cheap and not so easy to destroy: investing an action and a blood just to have the vampires pay a bit more for some cards cna be too much.

Acquired Ventrue Assets (37 decks)

A cheap and efficient way to bring back some blood on the table. Better in low or mid capacity decks with more Giovanni, even better if it can be defended a bit.

Trochomancy (35 decks)

A excellent bleed modifier, although the AN combination is more easily found among Harbingers of Skulls. For decks who can include it discipline-wise, it's a no-brainer.

Breath of Thanatos (31 decks)

Although less used than the defensive Spiritual Intervention, the aggravated damage at superior N is quite threatening. If the deck uses wraiths, the stealth provided at inferior n gets useful, and the card becomes very versatile.
