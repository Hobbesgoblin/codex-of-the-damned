Toreador

The Toreador and their antitribu are a prominent clan in tournaments. They gave birth to two archetypes: AAA using Toreador Camarilla Royalty and Jost Powerbleed using antitribu and their clan cards to deadly effects. Moreover, they are used in numbers in the Weenie AUS archetype.

Highlights

Anson (82 decks)

The definite star of the clan, Anson has a unique ability that can completely reshape the way Master cards are handled in a deck. Used alongside Ashur Tablets, he becomes very efficient.

Rafael de Corazon (65 decks)

With its superior O and D over its already powerful clan disciplines, he is the most played Inner Circle after Lutz von Hohenzollern, a vampire he often accompanies anyway.

Isabel de Leon (65 decks)

The cheapest A pool can buy. An easy inclusion in many constructs.

Anneke (56 decks)

Second "A" of the AAA archetype after Anson, her +1 bleed and unique ability to block cross table makes for nice package in any construct with intercept capabilities.

Art Museum (51 decks)

A nice bloat card: hard to remove and providing 1 pool per turn.

François Villon (47 decks)

Often side algonside Anson and Anneke, his title combined with his nice ability makes for a good option.

Alexandra (40 decks)

Last "A" of the AAA archetype alongisde Anson and Anneke, her bleed bonus and title come in handy and her special unlock ability is very strong.

Remilliard (41 decks)

A cheap A vampire with access to a useful inferior r, he gets included in many decks.

Andre LeRoux (37 decks)

His unique ability makes him a good addition in many construct hoping to get an open road for bleed actions at some point.

Marcellus (36 decks)

A decent price for a Pinrce with this AC disciplines. More often than not, he is seen using range weapons like .44 Magnum.

Delilah Easton (29 decks)

A cheap r vampire who finds her place in many decks.

Sheila Mezarin (29 decks)

Only 6 pool for a +1 bleed with AR, this is a bargain.

Palla Grande (29 decks)

The most played clan card for Toreador antitribu, it is an easy way to improve bleed for all vampires. The card is generally played in multiple copies in the deck, so as to make sure its destruction is not a stopping point for the construct.

Toreador Grand Ball (28 decks)

An iconic card of the Toreador clan, the cost of having a vampire locked is high, unless used in cunjonction with Alexandra. Anyway, even at this cost, making sure actions get through without even using stealth is very strong.

Jost Werner (27 decks)

The bonus stealth is quite strong combined with R, and the pool cost not so high.

Mariana Gilbert (25 decks)

A cheap price for superior R, she gets included in many decks.
