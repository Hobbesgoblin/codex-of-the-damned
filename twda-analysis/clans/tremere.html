Tremere

The Tremere and their antitribu have access to the unique and versatile t discipline. Alongside d and a, it makes for a powerful combination, used in many successful archetypes:

  • The War Chantry, a toolbox using all three clan disciplines adt.
  • Mistress, an Inner Circle build using Mistress Fanchon
  • The Nephandii, using Antonio d'Erlette to put multiple Nephandus into play
  • The Goratrix High Tower, a wall using Goratrix and its superior I as star.
  • Tupdogs, a rush archetype which uses Tremere antitribu to get some Tupdog into play and use them to dispose of the opponents minions.

Finally, Tremere also appear in the Tzimisce Wall archetype as both Mistress Fanchon and Malgorzata have V with powerful abilities.

Highlights

Mirror Walk (153 decks)

A good stealth enhancer, the only one available with the clan disciplines. Its combat avoidance effect at superior T can come in handy.

Magic of the Smith (140 decks)

A rare effect in the game, the ability to fetch any equipment in the library is very strong. The +3 stealth bonus at superior T even makes it very hard to block. Some decks even use T for this card only.

Keith Moody (123 decks)

The cheapest D pool can buy, he is played in many decks.

Theft of Vitae (86 decks)

An excellent combat card: as it steals blood there is no damage to prevent, and at the end of the round it is a 4 blood difference between the two vampires, at no cost.

Nephandus (76 decks)

An excellent ally: not so expensive, not unique so a multiple of them can be into play, they strike 1R with a press and prevent one damage per strike, so this means they do not loos any life against a vampire that does not play any combat card. They gave birth to the Nephandii archetype.

Ponticulus (73 decks)

A good ally at a cheap price: he is quite the defender and very resilient as aggravated damage are needed to kill him.

Rutor's Hand (73 decks)

An expensive but powerful device to augment ABC. Its drawback did not prevent it to be played in many t constructs, if only as an option.

Antonio d'Erlette (68 decks)

Second pillar of the Nephandii archetype, his capacity is exceptionnal. Being able to put some allies into play without even taking an action that could be blocked is a definitive advantage.

Arcane Library (63 decks)

A good bloat device at a decent price. Fex clans have access to this kind of location, but it generates a pool per turn so it is quickly profitable.

Mistress Fanchon (63 decks)

An Inner Circle with the classical 4 votes and +2 bleed bonus, she also has a unique ability to fetch the card she wants. Moreover, her ODA disciplines combination, alongside TV, is powerful in many decks.

Apportation (61 decks)

An excellent combat device: maneuver or press at no cost is a great tool.

Apportation (61 decks)

A star among the Tremere, her +1 intercept an good disciplines to cost ratio makes her a favorite in many decks.

Tupdog (57 decks)

An infamous rush device, only available as an offensive tool for Tremere antitribu. They gave birth to the Tupdog archetype.

Saiz (57 decks)

A good disciplines combination for only 3 pool, plus he is in the group 3 so can be included in many crypts.

Rego Motus (51 decks)

An excellent damage prevention card, even if not as powerful as f Rolling With the Punches.

Ember Wright (51 decks)

Yet another da for 3, included in many crypt for the same reason as Saiz: she's cheap and has good disciplines.
