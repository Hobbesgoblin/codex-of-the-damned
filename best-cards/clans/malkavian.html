Malkavian

The Malkavian are a prominent clan. With their unique e discipline and the excellent oa disciplines aside, plus some really good vampires, they have led to many archetypes. Lutz Politics and Rachel Madness are two archetypes revolving around the powerful vampires Lutz von Hohenzollern and Rachel Brandywine respectively. On the other hand, the Malkavian and their antitribu are also used into two strong weenie constructs: Weenie DEM and Weenie AUS.

Group 1 Malkavian have the doa disciplines combination, making them the only vampires of the game with do as clan disciplines. That got them into other archetypes, like the Mind Rape archetype, using Mind Rape to take control of opponents vampires. Malkavian and their antitribu got e instead of d since group 2, an evolution right on tracks with the roleplaying game Vampire: the Masquerade.

Highlights

Kindred Spirits

played in 99 decks, typically 7-21 copies

This bleed action is central to the e discipline. It can deliver a bleed of 2 in combination with a 1 pool bloat. Moreover, the bleed can be directed to any Methusalah, not only the prey, offering additional table control.

Lutz von Hohenzollern

(played in 94 decks, typically 1-4 copies

Star of the Malkavian clan, he has a really powerful ability, 4 votes and +2 bleed like many Inner Circle, combined with all clan disciplines plus the useful R that gives acces to excellent vote modifiers like Voter Captivation.

Confusion

played in 87 decks, typically 4-12 copies

An excellent bleed modifier at no cost, it provides stealth at superior E.

Eyes of Chaos

played in 83 decks, typically 4-11 copies

A powerful bleed enhancer at no cost, although a bit less efficient than Conditioning.

Zöe

played in 74 decks, typically 1-2 copies

Superior A at 3 pool is really a bargin. She has access to Telepathic Misdirection for the cheapest price.

Deny

played in 66 decks, typically 1-6 copies

A good stealth enhancer at superior E, its inferior ability is seldom used but can still come in handy to get out of a Trap, for example.

Madness Network

played in 47 decks, typically 2-5 copies

A very rare effect for this infamous clan card. It enables the Malkavian to stay in defence and only bleed at the end of the predator's turn, for example. But it can also be used to act multiple times during the successive turns of other players.

Uncle George

played in 46 decks, typically 1-2 copies

5 pool is a good price for the Eo combination. He is almost always paired with Persia in stealth & bleed constructs.

Muddled Vampire Hunter

played in 45 decks, typically 1 copy

An excellent ally with good combat abilities at a reasonable price. He can help take down a cumbersome minion or deter an aggressive predator.

Persia, The Beautiful Statue

played in 44 decks, typically 1 copy

Eo for 5 pool in the group 3, combined with a nice ability.

Apache Jones

played in 41 decks, typically 1-2 copies

5 pool is a good price for the Eo combination. He is almost always paired with Uncle George and Persia in stealth & bleed constructs.

Maris Streck

played in 40 decks, typically 2-4 copies

A Justicar with a very powerful ability she can use multiple times during an action. She gets included even in other clans crypts just for her ability and title.

Midget

played in 39 decks, typically 1-2 copies

3 pool is a low price to pay for the Eo combination. Its Infernal trait is a bummer, but he is still excellent for stealth & bleed constructs, where speed matters more than cost.

Touch of Clarity

played in 39 decks, typically 1-4 copies

The only card in the game that can counter d bounces like Deflection: it is a really powerful device in the e arsenal.

Victoria

played in 37 decks, typically 1-2 copies

Superior A for 5 pool gets her included in many crypts, often when o or c can be used.

The Call

played in 37 decks, typically 2-6 copies

A strong bloat device that can help get vampires out faster, especially at superior EA.

Jackie

played in 37 decks, typically 1-2 copies

The cheapest E of the game at only 3 pool, she gets included in many e decks.

Quentin King III

played in 34 decks, typically 1-2 copies

A Prince for 7 pool with a good disciplines combination. He is group 3, the same group as Maris Streck and is often included alongside her. The fact that he can use The Call and Madman's Quill is also a strong point.

Morel

played in 34 decks, typically 1-3 copies

All clan disciplines at superior for just 6 pool, this is a bargain.

Stavros

played in 33 decks, typically 1-3 copies

Although he does not have superior E, his superior R and title makes him a natural companion to Lutz von Hohenzollern in a political deck, or any OR political construct, for that matter.

Gilbert Duane

played in 32 decks, typically 1-3 copies

A prince for 7 and a very strong disciplines combination. He gets included in many Royalty decks.

Mind Tricks

played in 32 decks, typically 2-5 copies

+1 stealth for 1 blood: this is expensive. It is included in decks where o is not much available. Alongside Deny and Confusion, it can make for a very decent stealth & bleed module using just the E discipline.

Leandro

played in 31 decks, typically 1-3 copies

A strong Inner Circle with a good ability and Od, a good disciplines combination. He is seen in Malkavian Royalty builds but also in group 2 Inner Circle constructs like the Council of Doom archetype.

Tony

played in 28 decks, typically 1 copy

A good disciplines combination for 6. He can uses bounces, The Call and Madman's Quill, and superior E and inferior o is enough to be of use in a stealth & bleed deck.

Restructure

played in 28 decks, typically 1-2 copies

An excellent ally remover at superior E. It can get rid of the widespread Carlton Van Wyk and Ossian, who both become quickly problematic.

Madman's Quill

played in 25 decks, typically 2-4 copies

A bit pricey but its base effect can be worth it for a lunge. Its superior effect can get really efficient in some decks too.

Unmada

played in 25 decks, typically 2-4 copies

A strong ability for a Justicar, all for 9 pool. He is often seen in company with Lutz von Hohenzollern.

Ohanna

played in 24 decks, typically 1 copy

A really cheap 2 pool for d, she gets included in many crypts.
