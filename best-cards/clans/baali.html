Baali

Baali and their unique discipline y provide good clan cards. Their vampires are all Infernal, and 1 pool per turn is a high cost, but some of them, combined with the clan cards, have abilities matching this cost. There are multiple archetypes used by the Baali clan:

  • The Dark Side of Politics archetype, using Giotto Verducci and Kaymakli Fragment to get titled vampires into play fast and cheap.
  • The Girls Will Find Inner Circles archetype, using Cybele to get more Master actions.
  • The The unnamed archetype, a stealth & bleed construct starring, obviously, The unnamed.
  • The Council of Doom archetype, starring Huitzilopochtli along some Inner Circle vampires.

Highlights

Cybele

played in 65 decks, typically 1-7 copies

The star of the Baali clan, her ability led to Master-focused decks like Girls Will Find Inner Circles.

Veneficti

played in 57 decks, typically 1-2 copies

The best ally of the clan, Veneficti has a 2 points bleed combined with stealth, all for the cheap cost of 1 pool. Hard to resist: it is the kind of awesome cards one is paying the Infernal penalty for.

Tend the Flock

played in 45 decks, typically 3-8 copies

An excellent bloat device. Played in many Baali decks, it also plays well with Cybele ability. This card helps to make up for the Infernal penalty.

The Horde

played in 39 decks, typically 2-7 copies

An excellent sidekick - its ability can be used to boost older vampires bleed easily, even from torpor.

Arishat

played in 36 decks, typically 1-3 copies

An excellent vampire, with an excellent ability for politics, defensively or offensively, as she can use it multiple times during a single referendum.

The Unnamed

played in 34 decks, typically 4-5 copies

Star of his own archetype, his ability makes for a very comfortable bloat. His access to such a powerful disciplines combination allows him to use a broad range of cards.

Xeper, Sultan of Lepers

played in 34 decks, typically 1-3 copies

An excellent vampire, with a very annoying ability against ally constructs and a decent one for vote constructs. His inferior y is almost not an issue.

Annazir

played in 32 decks, typically 1-3 copies

Another excellent vampire, all clan disciplines at superior, votes and enhanced bleed, plus a nice ability that can become useful to get rid of a gun or a Bowl of Convergence.

Unleash Hell's Fury

played in 106 decks, typically 1-3 copies

The most played y card. The pool cost is steep, but it is a dreadful defence. It can be used to deter bleeds or protect vampires against aggressive actions. The threat of torpor without even a combat is hard to get around.

I am Legion

played in 54 decks, typically 1-4 copies

An excellent card against A bounces Telepathic Misdirection and My Enemy's Enemy, it does not jam the hand as it can be used at superior OY to just gain a pool for a blood (the card text states "and/or").

Sense the Sin

played in 52 decks, typically 2-5 copies

A very versatile card, it can be used for intercept or additional bleed, depending on the situation. Always useful, at no cost, even with no corruption counter around.

Psychomachia

played in 37 decks, typically 1-3 copies

A decent block denial, although limited to younger vampires and allies, it still applies to a lot of minions when one plays Cybele or The unnamed.

Contagion

played in 37 decks, typically 1 copy

A decent pool damage provider, although often played as a single copy in Baali decks.

Infernal Servitor

played in 20 decks, typically 2-4 copies

An excellent ally: although he does not stay in play, his bleed power is huge and can make for a very nice lunge.

Flames of the Netherworld

played in 16 decks, typically 1-4 copies

A very good combat strike. Sometimes even used with t, although the y version is more versatile.

Condemnation: Mute

played in 16 decks, typically 1-6 copies

An excellent card for politics, both defensively and offensively. Even Baali decks with no take on the politics game can play it just to hinder a politic opponent.
