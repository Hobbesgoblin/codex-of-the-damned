Archetypes

Bleed

Bleed decks are focused on prey aggression and use bleed actions to inflict heavy pool damages. d is without question the most damage-heavy bleed discipline, plus it does come with bounce cards. r and e are good second choices. This is the archetype with the most wins, scoring 2:1 over other archetypes in major tournaments.

Stealth & Bleed and Powerbleed

There are two ways to get the bleed through: using stealth is the most obvious one. o is the obvious stealth discipline, but it does not often come along with d. $ and j come second.

The other way to get the bleed through is to make sure the prey has no minion available to block the action. Along its strong bleed capabilities, d offers the excellent Seduction. O Elder Impersonation, N Call of the Hungry Dead, f Daring the Dawn and r Mind Numb are also effective tools. Combining these options with the few Master cards that can prevent block or lock minions — Pentex™ Subversion, Misdirection and Anarch Troublemaker — stealth is not an issue anymore.

Flavours

  • Lasombra and Kiasyd have a strong combination with d for bleed and $ for stealth. Moreover, they can use some Nocturn for defence or additional attack. $ also provides combat ends for protection with Oubliette and Shadow Body.
  • The Malkavian ’94 make use of group 1 Malkavian with the rare combination of d for bleed and o for stealth.
  • But the malkavian clan also gave birth to the Weenie DEM archetype, using low capacity vampires with just the e disciplines, which offers a good bleed and stealth combation by itself.
  • The Gangrel clan also gave birth to powerful bleed deck using group 2 with d Iliana, Ingrid Rossler and Stanislava. J provides good stealth with Earth Control, Form of Mist and Rapid Change.
  • Jost Werner gave birth to multiple build, thanks to his +1 stealth and RA combination. He can be associated with Rebekka as she has the same group, ability and disciplines combination, and/or be followed by fellow Toreador Antitribu ready to use Palla Grande and Art Scam. R delivers a solid powerbleed with Intimidation, Enchant Kindred, Aire of Elation and Mind Numb , and A gives access to a much needed bounce, Telepathic Misdirection
  • Inner Circles also deliver a very strong bleed as they have a +2 bleed ability, and often access to the D and O combination. Arika, Leandro, Mistress Fanchon and Rafael de Corazon have very similar disciplines, for example. Their big capacity makes it harder to get them out, and using Zillah's Valley and Information Highway, together with Villein or Minion Tap is common. A strong modern archetype, Girls will Find Inner Circle use them along Cybele and Aksinya Declau for a Multi Master Phase Actions (MMPA) variant based on the OR discipline combination. Using The Parthenon and more than a dozen Ashur Tablets and a lot of Liquidation, the decks keeps bloating and getting IC out at a steady pace until it crushes the table with the IC stealth bleed for 3.
  • The unnamed ability has been enough to give birth to an archetype by itself. Though he lacks d or A for bounces, he can produce a nice bloat by himself, more so once he gets the Enkil Cog + Homunculus combination out and begins to bleed twice per turn. With O and J, his stealth can be almost impossible to match, and he has access to Instantaneous Transformation for more actions, as well as Unleash Hell's Fury for defence.

Highlights

Bleed decks have the highest prey damage. They tend to oust their first prey quite often, but it also attracts the table attention on them and one oust is not enough for a game win. They are are usually weak in combat. They tend to rely on « combat ends » effects, but some flavours can have aggravated damage or torpor strikes as a threat.

Stealth rotation

Stealth & Bleed archetypes have a strong stealth module, but it can jam the hand. If the prey assumes she will not have the means to block, she should decide not to attempt to block at all, and try to jam her predator hand on the stealth module. Well constructed Stealth & Bleed decks include some rotation tools to help with that.

As they are focused on their prey, bleed decks have a weak defence against their predator. They usually use d or A for bounces, but their bloat options are often mediocre.

Vote

Vote decks usually have a strong pool damage module to deliver to their prey. The difficulty to gather enough votes to get the referendums through is balanced by the constant +1 stealth bonus of vote actions. There are basically two ways of building a vote deck: either breed and find a way to give votes to all your vampires, or use older vampires with titles themselves.

Flavours

  • Inner Circle can be used in strong vote decks. As in the build variant, getting the out is often done using Zillah's Valley and Information Highway, together with Villein or Minion Tap. On the vote topic, Lutz Von Hohenzollern deserves a special mention, as his ability and disciplines makes him perfect for the job. Many different decks have been built around him. An interesting variant, Summon the Elder, uses Al-Muntathir, God's Witness and its unique ! discipline to play Summon History then The Ankara Citadel, Turkey to get more IC into play faster, at half the price. the excellent Voter Captivation is then used to resplenish the vampires blood before going at it again, or using a Villein to bloat.
  • Brujah Princes also offer an excellent crypt for votes, especially with Dmitra Ilyanova. As for all Camarilla builds, their access to Parity Shift is a great asset, plus r action modifiers let them make sure the votes get through and provide the excellent Voter Captivation for bloating.
  • Balli offer a nice political angle: the Dark side of Politics archetype makes use of Kaymakli Fragment to get multiple Inner Circle or Justicars in play. Giotto Verducci is used to this effect, as his ability let him get the equipment easily.
  • The Legacy of Pander card made for an archetype by itself: playing it with low capacity Pander vampires that breed using The Embrace can get the vote lock soon and deliver a high amount of pool damage very fast. Moreover, having a swarm of vampires ensures Consanguineous Boon can be used for bloating very strong.
  • The Ravnos Clown Car is another interesting breed build. Getting low capacity Ravnos out, then having them use Mozambique Allure to become Laibon, then The Embrace to get more Ravnos out, makes for an incredible voteing power using then The Eldest are Kholo. Moreover, the Ravnos clan also has access to the Week of Nightmares.
  • Toreador antitribu also have a powerful political breed using Foundation Exhibit. Moreover, they also have access to the deadly Palla Grande and can combine the two masters to deadly effect.

Highlights

Votes decks deliver very high damage once they get the vote lock. They also have a good bloat, using Consanguineous Boon, Parity Shift, Ancient Inluence or Political Stranglehold depending on the build. Depending on the exact build, stealth vary, but they are not easily blocked. Builds that do not have high stealth levels rely on a swarm of minions that make it hard to block them all. Finally, vote decks have a very weak combat module and — especially the swarm builds — a weak bleed defence, relying exclusively on bounces, if at all.

Table Talk

Votes can go either ways and can be targeted effortlessly across the table. This gives unique negotiation options to vote decks, that can be used to keep a low profile and a lot of friends throughout the game. Table talk is alwys important in the game, but even more so for vote archetypes.

Wall

Wall decks maximise their defence, trying to block their prey and predator actions alike while slowly building up bloat and pool damages. The ability to intercept and wake are tantamount in these decks, so a and i are the most common options here. a gives more options and a higher intercept all in all, with the excellent Eyes of Argus atop. It’s also the only discipline apart d that provides bleed bounce reaction cards. i also has good intercept cards though, and better combat options than a: it can be used with no additional equipment or discipline. Sabbat, Camarilla and Laibon titled vampires each have access to excellent intercept cards: Eternal Vigilance, Under Siege, Second Tradition: Domain and No Secrets From the Magaji.

Flavours

  • Weenie walls are a standard build, playing low capacity vampires either with a and guns like Sniper Rifle and .44 Magnum, or with i and the versatile Deep Song. The number of vampires compensate their frailty — the deck may struggle against high levels of stealth, but can handle powerbleed or other weenie decks more easily.
  • The Tzimisce wall is a very classic build. The combination of both a & i allows for the use of Read the Winds, and high cap vampires can even use Sense the Savage Way. On top of Eyes of Argus this allows a build with a lot more wakes. Tzimisce can also use v for an aggravated damage combat module using Breath of the Dragon and Chiropteran Marauder, threatening to use Rötschreck if needed, as they usually enter combat as blockers and can therefore play an Out-of-Turn.
  • Tremere in general, but Goratrix in particular offer excellent wall capabilities. He has the same A & I disciplines as strong Tzimisce, and he can use T for combat with, but also to go fetch useful equipments with Magic of the Smith, like Heart of Nizchetus, Bowl of Convergence and Ivory Bow.
  • Ahrimanes also have good capabilities. The Cats archetype relies on Z and its excellent reactions: Speak With Spirits, Ears of the Hare and Falcon's Eye, in combination with I and its standard wall module.
  • Saulot & Capuchin provide a nice discipline combination, as they both have ATF and +1 bleed. Although they do not have i, T lets them fetch precious equipment early using Magic of the Smith like in the Goratrix build. F allows them a very resilient combat module based on damage prevention and Weighted Walking Stick.
  • Using the A & C to block and fight with Guns like Sniper Rifle and .44 Magnum is a common wall archetype that Toreador, Toreador Antitribu or Osebo crypts can deliver.

Highlights

Wall decks obviously have a strong defence and they're not easily taken down by their predator. Moreover, having high block capabilities let them play fragile master cards that need protection to be efficient like Smiling Jack, the Anarch, The Rack, Powerbase: Montreal, Powerbase: Barranquilla (Sabbat) and Anarch Revolt (Anarchs), helping both with bloat and offence. Still, wall decks tend to have a weak offence, dealing only a moderate amount of damage to their prey. They're very good at exhausting other decks around the table though, and will get their prey eventually if they manage to survive their predator.

Cross block

Eagle's Sight and Falcon's Eye are the only two cards in the game that let a vampire block any action, even if it is directed upon another Methusalah or undertaken cross table.

This ability to cross block is an excellent asset in wall decks with A or Z as it enhances table control and allows for example to intercept and dangerous vote from the grand-predator or a bleed from the prey that could be lethal to the grand-prey. Wall decks that do not use these will often include some rush capabilities instead.

Toolbox

Toolbox decks balance their offence and defence capabilities. They have a wall tendency, a decent combat module and real pool damage options to use on their prey, sometimes even some votes they can try to pass or tactical stealth options. The key to this archetype is to use cards offering multiple play options.

Flavours

  • Using Toreador Princes and Justicar led to the AAA archetype and its family. The name come from the vampires used in the crypt: Anson, Anneke and Alexandra. A variant uses only Anson and some sidekicks: the Anson guns archetype. Using a Prince and Justicar allows to play Second Tradition: Domain and Parity Shift. The combat module can use either r combat ends or c with guns. r delivers good bleed damage, espacially since Aire of Elation is stronger on a Toreador crypt. Anson can be the star of the deck for a Multi Master Phase Actions (MMPA) build. In this case, The Parthenon and a dozen of Ashur Tablets are included. On the other hand, having the Justicar and the Inner Circle in the crypt allows the play of Alastor along Assault Rifle to strengthen the c combat module. Finally, A is used for its bounces and additional intercept.
  • A few group 2 Brujah have the d discipline, and this led to the Euro Brujah archetype, using european Brujah Princes with d Constanza Vinti and Dònal O'Connor. They make good use of d with Govern the Unaligned and Deflection, with a bit of Conditioning for the lunge, and do some politics with Parity Shift and sometimes Archon. They rely on Second Tradition: Domain for blocks and pack a nasty PC combat module using Torn Signpost Pursuit, Immortal Grapple and Disarm.
  • The Resistência Anarch archetype uses Nana Buruku and her special along a low capacity i crypt with Anarch Convert and, of course, a dozen Ashur Tablets and some Liquidation. It has a good bloat, and uses the classic animalism combination of Aid from Bats and Carrion Crows for combat. The MMPA side of things allows to play a dozen Anarch Revolt and stack them quickly, then Deep Song can be used to eliminate the prey vampire who become Anarch.
  • The Nephandus led to a build of his own, along Antonio d'Erlette. The idea is to use this powerful ally in swarm, playing no-discipline combat cards like Target Vitals, Trap and Molotov Cocktail. The Unmasking is included for intercept, as well as Haven Uncovered for rush capabilities. Antonio d'Erlette is kept safe and can use Deflection for bounces. A variant uses a MMPA module with The Parthenon, more than a dozen Ashur Tabletsand some Liquidation. A recent brazilian variant uses Anarch Convert to be able to play Piper and put more Nephandus in play.
  • The Emerald Legion archetype uses the Emerald Legionnaire as the core of the deck, along some Liquidation to make the most of its ability. Using Harbinger of Skulls vampires led to solid defensive toolbox decks, with A for block and bounces, and f for combat resilience. Moreover, n has decent combat cards with Spiritual Intervention and Breath of Thanatos. The Vengeful Spirit completes the panel to offer decent bleed capability atop the grinding bleed for 1 of multiple Emerald Legionnaire. The Unmasking is used for additional intercept.
  • The Ventrue Antitribu Grinder build is a classic toolbox archetype. Using mid capacity vampires Owain Evans, The Wanderer and Blackhorse Tanner, plus some sidekicks, the DAF discipline combinations is a jack of all trades. Bounces, intercept, and resilient combat, using f prevention along Weighted Walking Stick and Target Vitals. Finally, the popular Govern the Unaligned helps getting the first vampires out cheap, then bring a strong bleed, often helped into that with D Conditioning, D Seduction and F Daring the Dawn. The idea is to slowly grind the blood and pool around the table to get to victory.
  • The True Brujah and their unique ? discipline brought an interesting toolbox build using Summon History. Two versions exist, one using Shalmath to act twice per turn, another using Lydia, Grand Praetor and Al-Muntathir, God's Witness, and their access to A for bounces and intercept. Summon History ignores requirements and allows to fetch clan-specific cards: The Ankara Citadel, Turkey first to get the following ones cheap, then Harzomatuili and Ponticulus for defence, sometimes Remnant of the Endless Storm for offence. Malajit Chandramouli is also included for more stealth. ! also offers the most nasty combat combination Domain of Evernight + Outside the Hourglass combination, inflicting two aggravated damages before strikes. This gets around most damage prevention, combat ends and dodges. The Lydia-Munthatir crypt usually uses Vaticination and Quicksilver Contemplation to discard bounces in the prey hand before bleeding strongly.

Highlights

Versatility is obviously the best asset of a toolbox deck. It can handle and exploit any table configuration. To make the most out of toolbox decks, one has to display excellent table awareness and negotiation abilities. For the toolbox to have a proper effect around the table and display all its efficiency, one needs to play their hand, whatever is in it. The deck chooses the strategy as much as the Methusalah. The player still chooses on which target to use its cards, but the cards in hand demand to be played for the deck to shine.

The Heart of Cheating

The Heart of Nizchetus is often included in toolbox builds, as it gives more control to the Methusalah over what his play can be. Its effect is makes such a difference in efficiency in such builds, it has been nicknamed The Heart of Cheating.

Finally, toolbox decks have strong defence, although not as strong as wall decks. They tend to have good intercept options and/or rush capabilities to handle their predator. Having much less offensive options than a vote or S&B deck, they cannot rely on pure speed to best their predator: they have to deter its actions actively using blocks, bounces or combats depending on the build.

Rush

Rush decks use combat as their main weapon. Many disciplines have good combat options. They are also good combat cards with no discipline. This is the archetype with the least wins in major tournaments, scoring 1:2 against other archetypes, 1:4 against bleed decks. Getting vampires down around the table is fun, but does not help so much to get the prey's pool down. To this effect, a handful of cards can help, namely Fame, Dragonbound and Tension in the Ranks.

Flavours

  • Tupdogs is a classic rush build, using a dozen Tupdog along a Tremere Antitribu, their low cost, rush ability, and powerful UP discipline combination allow for a devasting combat module using Immortal Grapple to prevent Combat Ends and Dodge, Brick by Brick to set the range, Raking Talons for aggravated damage and Stonestrength for prevention or press. As the Crow can even allow them to rush twice. Depending on the build, they can be accompanied by Rock Cat or Nephandus for support.
  • Samedi recently got a strong rush build, with Blessings of the Loa granting them much needed bleed power. H grants a very strong combat module with the excellent Relentless Reaper along Groaning Corpse and Necrosis.
  • The War Ghoul got a build of its own, using either low cap Tzimisce or the Little Tailor of Prague, together with cheap allies like Jake Washington or Underbridge Stray. Trap is even included for an even stronger combat.
  • The Guruhi provide a strong rush build too, with Nangila Were along Nana Buruku, sometimes followed by Fish or Enkidu, The Noah. The IP combination provides a very strong combat module, with Carrion Crows alongside Immortal Grapple and Torn Signpost. Drawing out the Beast, Slam and even Thrown Sewer Lid help manage long range combat modules. One strong side of the build is that Nana Buruku allows for a heavy use of masters, inluding more than a dozen Ashur Tablets for recursion.
  • Enkidu, The Noah led to a rush build of his own, often alongside Matasuntha. C let them use Psyche! and Pursuit, while Getting them anarch with Anarch Convert can let them use the very versatile Diversion and even Donnybrook. The JFC discipline combinations let them use both Forced March and Instantaneous Transformation to improve ABC. The deck also use some i cards to improve intercept and defence, mainly Raven Spy, Sense the Savage Way and Cats' Guidance.

Highlights

The ability to rush any minion provide these decks with a strong defence against their predator. Depending on the predator archetype, going against its minions first is a common strategy for a rush deck: it may take some combats to get them down, but they can disable their predator very fast. On the other hand, rush decks have a weak offence and have trouble finding a way to deliver pool damage to their prey. This is the core issue of the archetype and explains its low-grade results in tournaments.

Finnish Politics

If they do not take down their prey very fast, rush decks have a unique control over the table balance as they can enter combat with the minions of any Methusalah — and take them down. Proper table control is essential to the succes of such builds, but it also opens a unique strategy for negociation: using the threat of rush combat to get things out of your neighbours.

The finnish players have perfect this negociation strategy to such an extent, it is now known as "Finnish Politics" among the seasoned players.

Special

Some decks do not fit into any of the aforementioned archetypes. These rare constructs are unique combinations of library and crypt cards that proved unexpectedly efficient. The following examples are not exhaustive.

Flavours

  • The Cybelotron is a build created by Erik Torstensson in 2007 and refined by Ben Peal in 2007. It has a crypt of 12 Cybele, use Magic of the Smith to get a Soul Gem of Etrius out, add a Master: Discipline to get Cybele to 11 capacity, in order to be able to play Call the Great Beast, spawning the Great Beast immediatly and getting a fresh new Cybele out of the crypt, thanks to the Soul Gem. Golconda: Inner Peace and Redeem the Lost Soul are used for heavy bloating.
  • The Madness Reversal is a build created by Orian Gissler in 2009 and refined by Danilo Torrisi in 2015. It uses low capacity Malkavians along Madness Network to let them call the Reversal of Fortunes vote during other players turn to play twice or thrice as much as the rest of the table. The Parthenon and Brainwash are used to slow the neighbours game even more, Smiling Jack, the Anarch helps to kill the table faster.
  • The Baltimore Purge is a classic build, made quite stronger with the edition of Montano in 2016. The idea is to have the elder Lasombra play Baltimore Purge, then use Freak Drive and Graverobbing to steal its prey vampires. Mind Rape is played alongside, and eventually Heidelberg Castle, Germany helps make use of the blood on the stolen vampires. The rest of the deck is a standard and powerful Stealth & Bleed module fitting the Lasombra D£ disciplines.

Highlights

Special decks tend to uge complicated card combinations to a devastative effect. Their strenght is in the absolute advantage their combination provide if all gets well. Their weakness is the frailty of such a combination. Blocking the right action or countering the right Master can often stop them dead in their tracks. Moreover, known combinations tend to attract the attention of all players, often leading the Player to lose all its ally very early in the game.
