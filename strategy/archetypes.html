Archetypes

Bleed

Bleed decks are focused on prey aggression and use bleed actions to inflict heavy pool damages. d is without question the most damage-heavy bleed discipline, plus it does come with bounce cards. r and e are good second choices. This is the archetype with the most wins, scoring 2:1 over other archetypes in major tournaments.

Stealth & Bleed and Powerbleed

There are two ways to get the bleed through: using stealth is the most obvious one. o is the obvious stealth discipline, but it does not often come along with d. $ and j come second.

The other way to get the bleed through is to make sure the prey has no minion available to block the action. Along its strong bleed capabilities, d offers the excellent Seduction. O Elder Impersonation, N Call of the Hungry Dead, f Daring the Dawn and r Mind Numb are also effective tools. Combining these options with the few Master cards that can prevent block or lock minions — Pentex™ Subversion, Misdirection and Anarch Troublemaker — stealth is not an issue anymore.

Flavours

  • Lasombra and Kiasyd have a strong combination with d for bleed and $ for stealth. Check the Kiasyd Stealth & Bleed and Lasombra Nocturn archetypes.
  • The Malkavian can use e, alone or alongside o, for devstating bleeds, like in the Weenie DEM archetype.
  • Assamite also have a nice combination with q and o, as in the Black Hand archetype.
  • d also works well in weenie archetypes, like Weenie DOM and Bima Dominate.
  • The Gangrel clan also gave birth to powerful bleed deck using J for stealth, as in the Stanislava archetype and its variants.
  • Toreador with R and Ventrue with DRcan produce strong powerbleed decks like The Bleeding Vignes or Jost Powerbleed.
  • Inner Circles also deliver a very strong bleed as they have a +2 bleed ability. The Mistress archetype is a good example. They are often combined with Multi Master Phase Actions (MMPA), like in the Girls Will Find Inner Circle or Council of Doom archetypes.
  • Using J to bleed multiple times during the opponents turns with Homunculus is also powerful: The unnamed and Rachel Madness are built around this idea.

Highlights

Bleed decks have the highest prey damage. They tend to oust their first prey quite often, but it also attracts the table attention on them and one oust is not enough for a game win. They are are usually weak in combat. They tend to rely on « combat ends » effects, but some flavours can have aggravated damage or torpor strikes as a threat.

Stealth rotation

Stealth & Bleed archetypes have a strong stealth module, but it can jam the hand. If the prey assumes she will not have the means to block, she should decide not to attempt to block at all, and try to jam her predator hand on the stealth module. Well constructed Stealth & Bleed decks include some rotation tools to help with that.

As they are focused on their prey, bleed decks have a weak defence against their predator. They usually use d or A for bounces, but their bloat options are often mediocre.

Vote

Vote decks usually have a strong pool damage module to deliver to their prey. The difficulty to gather enough votes to get the referendums through is balanced by the constant +1 stealth bonus of vote actions. There are basically two ways of building a vote deck: either breed and find a way to give votes to all your vampires, or use older vampires with titles themselves.

Flavours

  • Inner Circle can be used in strong vote decks, as in The Dark Side of Politics and Lutz Politics
  • Camarilla Princes also often use votes, like in the Nosferatu Royalty archetype.
  • Non Camarilla vampires can also build good titled crypts, especially the Ishtarri with the Ishtarri Politics and Daughters of Cacophony, with Scout and Daughters Politics.
  • Balli offer a nice political angle with The Dark Side of Politics.
  • Assamite also have good options, like in the Amaravati Politics or the Death Star archetypes.
  • The Ravnos have a unique breed archetype: the Ravnos Clown Car.

Highlights

Votes decks deliver very high damage once they get the vote lock. They also have a good bloat, using Consanguineous Boon, Parity Shift, Ancient Inluence or Political Stranglehold depending on the build. Depending on the exact build, stealth vary, but they are not easily blocked. Builds that do not have high stealth levels rely on a swarm of minions that make it hard to block them all. Finally, vote decks have a very weak combat module and — especially the swarm builds — a weak bleed defence, relying exclusively on bounces, if at all.

Table Talk

Votes can go either ways and can be targeted effortlessly across the table. This gives unique negotiation options to vote decks, that can be used to keep a low profile and a lot of friends throughout the game. Table talk is alwys important in the game, but even more so for vote archetypes.

Wall

Wall decks maximise their defence, trying to block their prey and predator actions alike while slowly building up bloat and pool damages. The ability to intercept and wake are tantamount in these decks, so a and i are the most common options here. a gives more options and a higher intercept all in all, with the excellent Eyes of Argus atop. It’s also the only discipline apart d that provides bleed bounce reaction cards. i also has good intercept cards though, and better combat options than a: it can be used with no additional equipment or discipline. Sabbat, Camarilla and Laibon titled vampires each have access to excellent intercept cards: Eternal Vigilance, Under Siege, Second Tradition: Domain and No Secrets From the Magaji.

Flavours

  • A is the best intercept discipline, and the Weenie AUS wall is an obvious idea.
  • The Tzimisce Wall is also a very classic archetype, as the combination of a & i offers even better intercept capabilities.
  • Tremere in general, but Goratrix in particular offer excellent wall capabilities in the Goratrix High Tower archetype.
  • The The Cats uses Ahrimanes and their ZI disciplines combination.
  • Finally, Saulot & The Capuchin provide a nice discipline combination, as they both have ATF and +1 bleed. The Saulot & Friends archetype is built around them.

Highlights

Wall decks obviously have a strong defence and they're not easily taken down by their predator. Moreover, having high block capabilities let them play fragile master cards that need protection to be efficient like Smiling Jack, the Anarch, The Rack, Powerbase: Montreal, Powerbase: Barranquilla (Sabbat) and Anarch Revolt (Anarchs), helping both with bloat and offence. Still, wall decks tend to have a weak offence, dealing only a moderate amount of damage to their prey. They're very good at exhausting other decks around the table though, and will get their prey eventually if they manage to survive their predator.

Cross block

Eagle's Sight and Falcon's Eye are the only two cards in the game that let a vampire block any action, even if it is directed upon another Methusalah or undertaken cross table.

This ability to cross block is an excellent asset in wall decks with A or Z as it enhances table control and allows for example to intercept and dangerous vote from the grand-predator or a bleed from the prey that could be lethal to the grand-prey. Wall decks that do not use these will often include some rush capabilities instead.

Toolbox

Toolbox decks balance their offence and defence capabilities. They have a wall tendency, a decent combat module and real pool damage options to use on their prey, sometimes even some votes they can try to pass or tactical stealth options. The key to this archetype is to use cards offering multiple play options.

Flavours

  • Using Camarilla Princes and Justicars and their access to Second Tradition: Domain and Parity Shift led to good toolboxes like the AAA and Euro Brujah and Ventrue Royalty archetypes.
  • A lot of archetypes using allies also fall into this category, like Khazar's Diary, Emerald Legion and Nephandii.
  • The Ventrue antitribu also provide excellent toolboxes, this is the Anti Ventrue Grinder archetype and its variants.
  • The Tremere too have a good disciplines combination for a toolbox, as proves the War Chantry archetype.

Highlights

Versatility is obviously the best asset of a toolbox deck. It can handle and exploit any table configuration. To make the most out of toolbox decks, one has to display excellent table awareness and negotiation abilities. For the toolbox to have a proper effect around the table and display all its efficiency, one needs to play their hand, whatever is in it. The deck chooses the strategy as much as the Methusalah. The player still chooses on which target to use its cards, but the cards in hand demand to be played for the deck to shine.

The Heart of Cheating

The Heart of Nizchetus is often included in toolbox builds, as it gives more control to the Methusalah over what his play can be. Its effect is makes such a difference in efficiency in such builds, it has been nicknamed The Heart of Cheating.

Finally, toolbox decks have strong defence, although not as strong as wall decks. They tend to have good intercept options and/or rush capabilities to handle their predator. Having much less offensive options than a vote or S&B deck, they cannot rely on pure speed to best their predator: they have to deter its actions actively using blocks, bounces or combats depending on the build.

Rush

Rush decks use combat as their main weapon. Many disciplines have good combat options. They are also good combat cards with no discipline. This is the archetype with the least wins in major tournaments, scoring 1:2 against other archetypes, 1:4 against bleed decks. Getting vampires down around the table is fun, but does not help so much to get the prey's pool down. To this effect, a handful of cards can help, namely Fame, Dragonbound and Tension in the Ranks.

Flavours

  • The Tupdog archetype is a classic, using a dozen Tupdog along a Tremere Antitribu.
  • Allies with good combat capacities also make for good rush decks, like the War Ghouls and Shambling Hordes archetypes.
  • The Guruhi provide a strong rush build too, with their PI disciplines combination: The Guruhi Rush and Nananimalism archetypes.

Highlights

The ability to rush any minion provide these decks with a strong defence against their predator. Depending on the predator archetype, going against its minions first is a common strategy for a rush deck: it may take some combats to get them down, but they can disable their predator very fast. On the other hand, rush decks have a weak offence and have trouble finding a way to deliver pool damage to their prey. This is the core issue of the archetype and explains its low-grade results in tournaments.

Finnish Politics

If they do not take down their prey very fast, rush decks have a unique control over the table balance as they can enter combat with the minions of any Methusalah — and take them down. Proper table control is essential to the succes of such builds, but it also opens a unique strategy for negociation: using the threat of rush combat to get things out of your neighbours.

The finnish players have perfect this negociation strategy to such an extent, it is now known as "Finnish Politics" among the seasoned players.

Special

Some decks do not fit into any of the aforementioned archetypes. These rare constructs are unique combinations of library and crypt cards that proved unexpectedly efficient. The following examples are not exhaustive.

Flavours

  • Some archetypes focus on stealing the vampires of their opponents, like Mind Rape, Spirit Marionette and Baltimore Purge.
  • The Cybelotron is a build created by Erik Torstensson in 2007 and refined by Ben Peal in 2007. It uses a complicated combination to use Call the Great Beast efficiently.
  • The Madness Reversal is a build created by Orian Gissler in 2009 and refined by Danilo Torrisi in 2017. It uses Reversal of Fortunes to play twice or thrice as more as its opponents.

Highlights

Special decks tend to uge complicated card combinations to a devastative effect. Their strenght is in the absolute advantage their combination provide if all gets well. Their weakness is the frailty of such a combination. Blocking the right action or countering the right Master can often stop them dead in their tracks. Moreover, known combinations tend to attract the attention of all players, often leading the Player to lose all its ally very early in the game.
